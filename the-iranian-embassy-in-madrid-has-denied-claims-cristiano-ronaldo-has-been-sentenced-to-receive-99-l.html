Denial of Reports Regarding Cristiano Ronaldo’s Sentencing

Iranian Embassy in Madrid denies claims

The Iranian Embassy in Madrid has categorically denied reports that claimed footballer Cristiano Ronaldo was sentenced to the corporal punishment of ’99 lashes’. These reports circulated after a special moment between Ronaldo and a devoted fan, painter Fatemeh Hamami, led to rumors and misconceptions. However, the embassy has clarified that there is no truth to these claims.

Refworld describes “lashes” as a form of corporal punishment, involving a sharp slap or strike with a rope or whip. In Iran, this punishment is designated for particular offenses enumerated in the country’s Islamic penal code. Adultery, as defined by Iranian law, includes touching a married woman without proper consent. Those found guilty of adultery can be sentenced to receive 99 lashes as punishment.

Several news outlets relayed claims from Iran that Ronaldo was subject to this punishment after a picture of him with a female fan sparked controversy. It was even suggested that he would be arrested and forced to serve a sentence if he ever set foot in Iran again. However, the Iranian Embassy in Madrid has categorically denied these claims, stating that no sentence has been issued against any international athlete in Iran.

The embassy’s statement emphasized that Cristiano Ronaldo traveled to Iran on September 18 and 19 to play in an official soccer match and was warmly received by the people and authorities.
