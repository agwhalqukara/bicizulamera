Eva Longoria Shares Video Trampolining With Celeb Friends Celebwell

September 2024 · 3 minute read

Eva Longoria continues to jump away to her strongest body ever. The 46-year-old actress, who has been showing off her Jumpsport trampoline on social media for almost a year now, was joined by fellow thespians including Sophia Bush, Jenna Ushkowitz, and Diana Maria Riva, at a trampoline exercise class hosted by the Los Angeles branch of The Ness and taught by Aly Giampolo, Eva's go-to trampoline trainer. Of course, all of the fit ladies shared photos and videos of the fun fitness class on social media. In addition to the "serious" workout video posted by The Ness, a few of the ladies shared their own outtakes. "When the coffee kicks in," Eva captioned one, featuring her dancing like crazy on her Jumpsport in a taupe Body Language workout outfit

In late 2020, Eva first started jumping her way into fitness when she was gifted a Jumpsport from one of her pals. "New Trampoline!!" she captioned a video. "My girl @elsamariecollins gave me a new trampoline from @jumpsportfitness @thenessnyc and it's amazing!!! Thank you Elsa! Best workout ever!"

Eva regularly relies on Aly Giampolo, founder of trampoline studio The Ness, for trampoline fitness classes. "Saturday Bounce! Claudia and I trying to keep up with our girl @alygiampolo from @thenessnyc. Can't live without my trampoline!!" she captioned an Instagram video

The majority of Eva's workout clips take place on her trampoline. She uses it for side lunges to weight lifting and squats. 

Using a trampoline is good for the knees, per Eva. "Oh my god this feels amazing," Eva explained in one of her Instagram videos about the trampoline. "It actually feels better on my knees."

According to a NASA study published in the Journal of Applied Physiology, rebounding exercise is 68 percent more efficient than jogging. And, according to a study by the American Council on Exercise mini trampoline workouts e burn an average of 12.4 calories per minute for men and 9.4 calories per minute for women. 

A 2016 in the Journal of Sport and Health Science found that trampoline jumping also helps with bone density and strength.
